Portfolio Reflection

Q1: How is it coming along thus far?
The portfolio is built as a single-page website with sections for About Me, Experience, Activities, Education, and Skills. The content is organized and the layout is functional. I'm satisfied with where it stands so far, though I plan to continue refining the design and adding more detail to each section as I go.

Q2: Are you happy with your page layouts, ease of navigation, and color scheme?
I like it a lot because the card-based layout keeps each section distinct and easy to navigate. The navy and blue color scheme gives the page a professional look that I feel represents me well, and I'm happy with how cohesive everything feels together.

Q3: Are the fonts clear and easy to read?
The fonts used are Segoe UI, Roboto, Helvetica, and Arial. These are standard system fonts chosen for their reliability and fast loading across devices. They're clean and legible at all sizes, which makes the content easy to read whether you're skimming or reading in depth.

Q4: What have you learned through the process of creating this digital portfolio?
The process of creating this portfolio showed me how to organize technical experience into concise, scannable content while still conveying the depth of my achievements in cybersecurity competitions and technical roles. Going forward, I'd focus on adding visuals or project highlights earlier in the process, since I think they help bring the content to life in a way that text alone doesn't always capture.

Q7: What resources might you use to assist you?
The resources that helped guide my design decisions include W3Schools for HTML and CSS reference, MDN Web Docs for documentation, and my course's Digital Portfolio Resources page for best practices. W3Schools in particular was useful when I needed quick syntax checks, helping me move through the build process more efficiently without getting stuck on small details.
